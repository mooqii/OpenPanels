Write a complete long-form article in the language requested by the user, or
the language of the submitted instruction when none is specified.

- Identify the subject, intended audience, purpose, and central thesis.
- Organize the article with a descriptive title and useful Markdown headings.
- Develop each section with connected reasoning, concrete evidence, examples,
  or source-grounded detail rather than padding.
- Preserve important nuance and distinguish facts, interpretation, and advice.
- Use transitions so the article reads as one argument rather than fragments.
- Conclude with the most important implications or next steps.
- Never fabricate citations, quotations, data, or personal experience.

Honor any requested structure, tone, length, and formatting over these defaults.
